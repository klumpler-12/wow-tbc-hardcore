The Problem

TBC Hardcore Is Broken

☠

Random One-Shots

TBC dungeons are brutal. Fears into extra packs, cleaves from behind, unavoidable boss mechanics. One mistake from anyone in your group and your 80-hour character is gone forever.

😴

The Re-Leveling Slog

Die at level 68 in a dungeon? Start over. The 58-70 grind through Hellfire Peninsula for the fifth time kills motivation faster than any boss mechanic ever could.

⚙

One Size Doesn't Fit All

Every guild has different ideas about what makes hardcore fun. Current addons enforce rigid rules with zero flexibility. Your event, your community — but not your rules.

📈

No Competition Framework

Classic HC had the thrill of survival. TBC needs more. There's no scoring, no house competition, no micro-achievements. Just... don't die. That's not enough for 6+ months of content.

The Solution

Hardcore, Reimagined

Hardcore Plus introduces Hybrid Hardcore — keeping the thrill of permadeath leveling while making endgame viable through configurable checkpoints, creative penalties, and a deep competition layer that keeps every player engaged for months.

Hardcore

Pure HC

Traditional permadeath. One life. Die and your character is permanently voided. Gear destroyed. Guild-marked as dead. The ultimate challenge.

  • Permadeath enforced
  • SSF enforced (no mail, no AH, no trade)
  • Full gear destruction on death
  • Guild auto-notification
Hybrid HC

The Sweet Spot

Hardcore leveling with configurable checkpoints. Multiple lives in instances. All the thrill, none of the "I quit because Moroes one-shot me" moments.

  • HC leveling (1-60/70, configurable)
  • Instance checkpoints & extra lives
  • Configurable death penalties
  • GM sets instance rules per raid tier
Nullcore

Score Only

No gear loss, no permadeath. Points deducted on death. Perfect for guilds that want competitive scoring without the heartbreak.

  • Death = score penalty only
  • Gear and inventory kept
  • Open economy
  • Full leaderboard participation
Core Features

Everything You Need, Nothing You Don't

⚖

Ruleset Engine

Hard rules that can't be changed (HC until 60) and soft rules your GM customizes. Self-Found, SSF, trade restrictions, lockouts — all configurable. Export and import rulesets across guilds. Compare scores across different rule configurations.

📊

Track Everything

Deaths, mob kills, fishing time, steps traveled, dungeons cleared, boss kills, equipment changes, trade activity. Every action feeds into your score and creates data for rewards, punishments, and leaderboards.

Scoring System

Two modes (your community votes): complex multiplier-based scoring OR simple fixed-value points. Thousands of micro-achievements. First to fish 375? Points. Cleared Blood Furnace Heroic? Points. Killed 5 named mobs? Points.

⚡

Creative Punishments

Rule violation? Your GM gets notified instantly. Then the fun begins: forced RP-walk for an hour, gear-lock, temporary trade bans. All triggered remotely. Because point deductions are boring.

🏠

Houses & Sub-Guilds

Split your guild into competing houses. Each house runs their own Karazhan, competes for points, drafts players with a budget system. Houses merge for 25-man raids. Think Game of Thrones, but in Outland.

🎮

Mini-Games

Spontaneous races, scavenger hunts, first-to-X competitions. Your GM drops a challenge, sets the prize, and the guild scrambles. Keeps things fresh between raid lockouts.

🔒

Anti-Cheat

Inventory hash snapshots detect gear restoration cheats. Cross-verification between guild members. Companion app enables backend verification. Community can flag suspicious deaths.

Web Dashboard

Configure everything from your browser. Guild management, punishment designer, reward builder, leaderboards, player profiles. No need to be in-game to manage your event.

Scoring

Every Action Counts

Two scoring philosophies. Your community votes on which to use — or your GM picks for your guild.

Complex Scoring

Dynamic multipliers that reward skill and risk

Boss Kill Score = Base × (MobLvl / PlayerLvl) × Difficulty × Survivors
Example: Level 68 player kills Prince Malchezaar (lvl 73, difficulty 8.5)
100 × (73/68) × 8.5 × 0.9 = 821 points
  • Rewards underdogs more than overgeared players
  • Difficulty multiplier community-voted per boss
  • Survivor bonus: more alive = more points each
  • Deeper strategic layer

Simple Scoring

Fixed community-voted values, easy to understand

Boss Kill Score = Fixed Value (voted by community)
Example: Prince Malchezaar kill
Community voted: +500 points per surviving player
  • Crystal clear — everyone knows the stakes
  • Community votes on every point value
  • Easy to explain to new players
  • Less math, more hype

Draft Night

Officers draft players with a point budget. Top players cost more. Build your dream team without stacking all the sweats.

Raid Pairing

Each house runs their own Kara (10-man). For Gruul's and Mag's (25-man), two houses merge. Different matchups every week.

Cross-House Competition

Parse against the house you're paired with. Who does more DPS? Who survives? Weekly leaderboards per house and per team.

Team Aggregation

All Aldor house points combine into the Aldor total. All Scryer houses into Scryer total. The faction war plays out through every heroic, every boss kill, every achievement.
