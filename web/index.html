Four Ways to Play

From classic permadeath to competitive leagues — pick the mode that fits your playstyle. Each brings unique mechanics to TBC.

💀

Hardcore Classic

The original ruleset. One life, one character. Death is permanent and absolute.

⚰️ Permadeath — character is voided forever on any death
🔒 Full SSF — no mail, no AH, no trading, no grouping outside dungeons
🚫 No boosts, no shortcuts — level 1 to 70 every time
📜 Closest to official Classic HC rules, adapted for TBC
Severity
⚡

Hardcore Plus

Permadeath with progression. Hit checkpoints to unlock boosts for future characters.

⚰️ Permadeath — same finality as HC Classic
🔓 Checkpoint boost: reach level 70 once → unlock 58 boost for future characters
📊 Account-wide scoring — earn points for quests, milestones, achievements
🏅 Leaderboards and seasonal rankings with titles
Severity
🔥

Softcore

You survive death, but lose everything equipped. Rebuild from your bank and keep going.

💔 All equipped gear, inventory, and gold destroyed on death
📦 Bank and alts are safe — rebuild from your stash
🤝 Guild trading allowed — partial SSF with trusted allies
🔄 No character deletion — death is costly but not final
Severity
🏆

Nullcore League

No permadeath. Pure score-based competition where every action earns or costs points.

📊 Deaths deduct points — quests, dungeons, achievements earn points
🏁 Season-based competition with time-limited leaderboards
🎖️ Tier titles: Bronze → Silver → Gold → Gladiator
🌍 No trade restrictions — play WoW normally, compete on merit
Severity
📡 Death Monitoring
